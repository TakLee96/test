Hero City Hack

11/8/2014

Hero City Hack is a 24-hour hackathon that happens at Hero City, Draper University. The main focus of the hackathon is bitcoin, health and fitness, and business.

Our Product: BitRun

Team Members: Me, Philip and Ryan

BitRun has a web application and also an iPhone front end. The basic mechanism of this application is that the user sets a goal for himself and bet certain amount of money in bitcoin to motivate himself to achieve the goal. If he fails, he will lose the money; otherwise, he will win the money back once he achieves his goal. Our initial design is to let this app help people achieve the goal of running certain distance, and the iPhone app will receive data from the iPhone motion chip to verify that the users indeed did this distance of running; the web front end is just used for register account, display data in real time and long run, and set up goals. I mainly work on the web front end with HTML, CSS, Vanilla JavaScript, and some Bootstrap. AND it's also the first time I try to use the React.js framework. (In fact I just learnt about it one day before the hackathon).

Bay BitHack

11/1/2014

Bay BitHack is a 24-hour long Hackathon dedicated to Bitcoin.

Our Product: TxtCoin

Team Members: Me, Philip

My partner and I build a Back-End system to support creating Bitcoin account, receiving QR code image for wallet address, and sending and receiving Bitcoin payments only through text messages: (415) 691-2236. It is mainly based on the twilio message API, Node.js and the BlockChain.info API for manipulating bitcoin transactions. I was quite impressed by the complicatedness of bitcoin but also its very unique and exciting design, and I also learnt a lot about back-end architecture and also error-handling techniques.
